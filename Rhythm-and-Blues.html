Genre Analysis

An Overview of R&B

R&B (Rhythm and Blues) is a very diverse genre of music. The term dated back to the 1940s and began to describe all African American music that was secular. African American swing, jazz, pop, and blues influenced this genre and shaped what we hear today. Additionally, gospel music served as a huge source of inspiration for this genre. R&B emphasized rhythms over the instrumental and harmonic nature of swing orchestras. It adopts emotionally driven vocalists, which prompted many acapella groups and acts. This genre is significant to society because it helped grow African American culture and promote racial acceptance and integration in a society of white people. It also influenced future music styles in society, such as rock and roll.
